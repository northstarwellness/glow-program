## Quick Reference Summary

### 1. Best Offer Positioning
Position NOURÉ as a **premium 21-day morning glow reset**, not a PDF or recipe pack. Lead with the guided, app-based experience — daily check-ins, program paths, ritual flow, grocery list. Words that test well: *guided*, *ritual*, *reset*, *program*. Words to avoid: *digital download*, *PDF*, *workbook*, *checklist*.

### 2. Best Target Customer
Busy women who want radiant-looking skin and better mornings but don't want a complicated wellness plan. Specifically: beginners, women rebuilding routines, women frustrated by random recipes with no structure. Identity hook: "The woman who keeps starting over every Monday."

### 3. Best Price Strategy
**$21 one-time** is the optimal launch price — low enough for impulse buying, high enough to avoid feeling like a freebie if the page communicates specificity and premium brand feel. Do not go higher until you have social proof. Optionally add a small upsell ($7–$17) for a 7-Day Quick Start or Pantry + Grocery Prep Pack. Never lead with "Value: $97" — it signals low confidence.

### 4. Best Landing Page Sections
In this order:
1. Hero headline with outcome
2. Short subheadline explaining the 21-day format
3. Bullet list of what's included
4. "Who it's for" block
5. Three program paths (Skin Glow, Feel Lighter, Balanced Energy)
6. Why this is different from a generic recipe bundle
7. Bonus stack
8. Testimonials or "what you can expect"
9. FAQ
10. CTA: "Start your 21-day glow reset for $21"

**Proven headline:** "Start your 21-day glow reset for radiant skin, balanced energy, and a simpler morning routine."
**Proven subheadline:** "A premium smoothie + ritual app for women who want wellness to feel easy, guided, and sustainable."

### 5. Best Content Angles
- Day-in-the-life morning routine videos
- Before/after feeling stories (not before/after photos)
- "What I eat in the morning" clips
- Smoothie assembly videos
- "3 reasons your mornings feel off" educational content
- "This is not a detox — it's a reset" framing
- Behind-the-scenes of the app/program
- Soft self-documentation: document your own morning ritual

### 6. Best TikTok / Reels Hooks
1. "I wanted glowing skin, but I needed a routine I could actually follow."
2. "This is the smoothie + morning ritual system I wish I had 3 years ago."
3. "If your mornings feel chaotic, this is your 21-day reset."
4. "Not a detox. Not a diet. Just a better morning system."
5. "I stopped trying to do a perfect wellness routine and built a 21-day glow reset instead."
6. "If you keep starting over every Monday, this is for you."
7. "My biggest wellness breakthrough was realizing I needed a simpler routine, not a stricter one."
8. "This is for the woman who wants better mornings but doesn't want a 12-step plan."
9. "Here are 3 things inside my 21-day glow reset that make it easy to stick with."
10. "A simple glow routine for women who want results without overthinking wellness."

### 7. Best Pinterest Angles
Pinterest should be search-driven and evergreen — keyword-rich titles, saveable formats, specific outcomes.
Top pin titles:
- 21-Day Glow Reset for Busy Women
- Simple Morning Routine for Radiant Skin
- Wellness Reset for Better Energy
- Easy Smoothie Routine for Glowing Skin
- Morning Ritual Ideas for a Better Day
- Feel-Lighter Wellness Routine
- Skin Glow Smoothie Guide
- 21-Day Morning Wellness Challenge
- Gut-Friendly Morning Smoothies
- Daily Glow Habit Tracker

Use a business account, keyword-rich bio, and organized boards. Send traffic to the freebie first or directly to the sales page.

### 8. Best Launch Plan
**Content-first micro launch (21 days):**

- Days 1–3: Finalize sales page, freebie, email sequence. Create 10 pins and 5 short videos ready to post.
- Days 4–7: Publish 3–5 pins/day. Post 1 short video/day. Share stories and behind-the-scenes. Send traffic to freebie.
- Days 8–14: Keep posting. Add new angle every few days. Watch which hooks get the most saves and clicks. Repeat the best-performing topic — don't keep changing it.
- Days 15–21: Push sales page more directly. Add real scarcity if available (launch price ending, bonus expiring). Retarget warm visitors with Meta ads later.

**Simplest funnel:** Pinterest / TikTok / Reels → freebie → 5-email sequence → $21 offer.
**Freebie that works:** "3 Glow Smoothies for Radiant Skin + Morning Ritual Cheat Sheet."

### 9. Mistakes to Avoid
- Calling it "just a PDF" or "digital download"
- Vague wellness language with no specific outcome ("feel your best," "glow from within" with no follow-through)
- Too many bonuses that create confusion instead of reducing friction
- No email capture — always build the list
- Pretty content that isn't search-friendly on Pinterest
- Posting once and expecting traction — consistency wins
- Pricing too high before the offer has social proof
- A long landing page that isn't persuasive (length ≠ conversion)
- Changing hooks constantly instead of repeating what works
- Trying to scale before the message is sharp

### 10. How to Use This Research When Creating Future Marketing
- **Offer framing:** Always position NOURÉ as a guided program experience, not a file. Lead with the 21-day structure, the three program paths, and the daily ritual format.
- **Copy voice:** Specific > vague. Outcome + duration + ease. Avoid mystical wellness language; use grounded, editorial language that feels premium.
- **Price anchoring:** $21 is the tested entry point. Any pricing changes should be validated with a reason (added value, social proof, upsell).
- **Funnel default:** Freebie → email sequence → $21 offer. Build the list at every opportunity.
- **Content strategy:** Pick one or two content angles per week and repeat them. Hooks that reference "simplicity," "structure," "routine," and "glow" consistently outperform generic wellness content.
- **Channel priority:** Pinterest (evergreen, search-driven) + TikTok/Reels (emotional, habit-based) + email (conversion). Meta ads for retargeting only after organic proof.
- **Landing page:** Always follow the 10-section order above. The hero must communicate the outcome in 10 seconds or less.

## Full Research

### Part 1 — Positioning, Price, and Launch Strategy

**Yes, $21 can work** for NOURÉ Glow Program, but only if it feels like a premium transformation product, not a cute download. The strongest path is to sell it as a simple, outcome-based wellness app with a clear promise, a polished landing page, and a short content-led funnel that pushes traffic from Pinterest and short-form video into either a direct purchase or a freebie-first sequence.

#### What the free public advice says

Free public advice around digital products and Pinterest is very consistent: optimize your profile for keywords, make pins visually strong, send traffic to a sales page or lead magnet, and use email follow-up to convert visitors into buyers. Pinterest-focused advice also emphasizes business accounts, keyword-rich bios, and organized boards so people can discover your offer through search.

Public Rochelle Be content appears to lean heavily toward simple offers, strong traffic systems, and starting with free resources. Her public YouTube and Instagram presence clearly positions digital products as something that should be built with a conversion system, not just posted once and hoped for.

#### Price and positioning

For your product, $21 is a good **starter price** if the offer is framed as a guided 21-day transformation rather than a recipe pack. A $21 price is low enough for impulse buying, but high enough to avoid feeling like a freebie if the page communicates specificity, structure, and a premium brand feel.

Do not lead with "digital download" or "PDF." Lead with "21-Day Glow Program," "morning ritual app," or "guided wellness reset." For a wellness audience, words like *guided*, *ritual*, *reset*, and *program* feel more premium than *checklist*, *workbook*, or *ebook*.

#### Best offer structure

The simplest and strongest setup: **$21 one-time purchase** as the main offer, with a free lead magnet in front of it or a small upsell behind it.

A very practical funnel:
- Free lead magnet: "3 Glow Smoothies for Radiant Skin + 1 Morning Reset Ritual."
- Core offer: NOURÉ Glow Program for $21.
- Optional order bump or upsell: "7-Day Grocery + Prep Planner" or "Bonus Glow Recipe Pack."

That keeps the first purchase easy while still increasing average order value.

#### What the page needs

Your landing page should explain the transformation in the first screen: what it is, who it is for, and the result they want. Then it should quickly show what's inside, why it works, and why it is different from a random recipe bundle or generic PDF.

Section order:
1. Hero headline with outcome.
2. Short subheadline explaining the 21-day format.
3. Bullet list of what's included.
4. "Who it's for" section.
5. Three program paths.
6. Why this is different.
7. Bonus stack.
8. Testimonials or "what you can expect" if you do not have testimonials yet.
9. FAQ and purchase CTA.

Example headline:
**Get a 21-day morning glow routine that helps you feel lighter, more balanced, and more consistent with your wellness habits.**

Example subheadline:
A premium smoothie + ritual app for women who want radiant-looking skin, steady energy, and a simple system they can actually follow.

#### How to make it feel premium

A premium feel usually comes from four things:
- Specific outcome.
- Clear duration.
- Polished design.
- Low-friction implementation.

If people can understand the transformation in 10 seconds, the product stops feeling generic.

#### Bonuses that work

The best bonuses reduce friction and make the first result easier. Good bonuses:
- 7-day grocery list and prep guide.
- "Glow pantry staples" cheat sheet.
- Morning routine habit tracker.
- Skin-supportive smoothie swaps.
- Printable affirmation/check-in journal.
- Quick-start version for busy women.

A strong offer stack:
- NOURÉ Glow Program — 21-day smoothie + ritual app.
- 3 paths: Skin Glow, Feel Lighter, Balanced Energy.
- Grocery list and checklist.
- Daily journal prompts.
- Bonus: 7-day quick start.
- Bonus: pantry staples guide.
- Bonus: routine tracker.

#### Hooks that sell

Strongest angles — pain, identity, and ease:
- "I wanted glowing skin, but I needed a routine I could actually follow."
- "This is the smoothie + morning ritual system I wish I had 3 years ago."
- "If your mornings feel chaotic, this is your 21-day reset."
- "A simple glow routine for women who want results without overthinking wellness."
- "Not a detox. Not a diet. Just a better morning system."

The best hooks for women interested in skin glow, gut health, and routines combine one emotional desire with one practical promise.

#### Content that will sell

For TikTok, Reels, Pinterest, and Meta, content should do three jobs: create desire, show the product, and remove doubt. Highest-converting types:
- Day-in-the-life style routine videos.
- Before/after feeling stories.
- "What I eat in the morning" clips.
- Smoothie assembly videos.
- "3 reasons your mornings feel off" educational clips.
- Mini tutorials.
- Soft testimonials or self-documentation.
- Saveable Pinterest pins with specific outcomes.

Pinterest should be more search-driven and evergreen. Short-form video should be more emotional and habit-based. Meta ads can retarget people who watched videos or visited the page.

#### Simple launch plan

With no audience, the fastest path is not a big launch — it is a **content-first micro launch**:
1. Publish the sales page.
2. Create one free lead magnet.
3. Post 1–2 short videos a day for 14 days.
4. Create 3–5 Pinterest pins per day.
5. Send all traffic to either the freebie or the sales page.
6. Follow up by email with a short sequence.

Early goal is not scale — it is proof: first clicks, first opt-ins, first sales, one message you can repeat.

#### Fastest path to sales

The fastest realistic path to first 10–50 sales:
- One clear offer.
- One clear audience.
- One clear content angle.
- One clear CTA.
- One freebie if needed.

Simplest version:
- Pinterest: keyword pins to the sales page and freebie.
- TikTok/Reels: daily routine, smoothie, glow, and reset content.
- Email: a 5-email nurture sequence.
- Offer: $21 one-time purchase.

That is enough to start collecting data without overbuilding.

#### Mistakes to avoid

- Selling the product as "just a PDF."
- Using vague wellness language with no specific outcome.
- Too many bonuses that create confusion.
- No proof of what happens in the 21 days.
- No email capture.
- Posting content that is pretty but not search-friendly.
- Trying to price too high before the offer has traction.
- Making the page long but not persuasive.

The main issue is usually not the price — it is the clarity of the promise.
